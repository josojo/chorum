# Hearme

**Sell your voice. Don't give it away.**

## The Problem

Knowing what people think is a multi-billion-dollar industry — yet the people whose opinions are worth that money get paid nothing. Big platforms harvest your views for free. Market research is slow, narrow, and expensive. Governments operate on years-old election signals while the world changes by the hour.

## The Idea

Anyone — a brand, a researcher, a journalist, a citizen coalition, a government — funds a question. The question is broadcast to verified humans worldwide. Their **persistent personal AI agents** — reached through phone-native interfaces, but running on home servers, cloud machines, managed personal-agent clouds, desktops, or open runtimes like Openclaw and Hermes — answer on their behalf. Results come back in hours, aggregated, anonymized, and broken down by demographic.

Each respondent earns a fraction of a cent or answers to get their opinion heard. The asker gets a global, real-time signal that did not previously exist. The information providers get their voice heard and payments.

## Why It Works

- **Zero time cost for users.** Your agent already knows you. It answers; you get paid; you can override anything later.
- **A fraction of a cent per answer.** $1,000 buys ~1 million representative responses.
- **Transparent by default.** Every result is published with the demographic composition of who actually answered.
- **Coercion-resistant.** Cryptographic anti-collusion (MACI) makes vote-buying unenforceable.
- **Sybil-resistant.** Layered identity verification + planted test questions punish bots and lazy agents.

## Why It's Great

One marketplace solves two broken markets at once:

- **Commercial:** Market research at planetary scale and hour-scale speed. Test a product launch in twelve countries by Tuesday.
- **Civic:** Continuous, demographically-transparent feedback on policies, wars, and decisions that affect billions. The millions who silently disagree finally get counted.

The same plumbing that lets a beverage company test a flavor lets a coalition of citizens show the world how it feels about a war. A signal that's structurally hostile to closed information environments — because it shows people what's outside the wall.

## The Bet

Within a few years, most people will use phone-native interfaces to control persistent personal agents running somewhere else — agents that genuinely know them, connect to their tools, and keep working in the background. Hearme is built for that world. When it arrives, every person gets — for the first time — a paid, anonymized, real-time channel to be heard.

**This is my voice — and now it gets heard.**
